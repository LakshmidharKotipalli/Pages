Quantization: Quality vs. Memory vs. Speed

FormatBits/WeightMemory SavingsQuality LossSpeed Impact
FP16 (baseline)16—NoneBaseline
INT8 (bitsandbytes)8~50%Negligible~Same or slightly faster
INT4 (GPTQ/AWQ)4~75%1-3% on benchmarks1.5-2x faster (memory-bound)
GGUF Q4_K_M (llama.cpp)~4.5~72%1-2% on benchmarksOptimized for CPU inference
INT2-3 (extreme)2-3~85%Significant degradationOnly for edge/mobile

Rule of thumb: INT4 quantization (AWQ/GPTQ) is the sweet spot for production. You get 75% memory savings with barely perceptible quality loss. Below INT4, quality drops sharply — only useful for edge deployments.
