Related Topics to Explore

1. Vector Databases Deep Dive

Connection to RAG: Vector databases are RAG's foundation

What to learn:

  • Vector indexing algorithms (HNSW, IVF, PQ)
  • Database operations (sharding, replication)
  • Performance tuning and optimization
  • Vector database alternatives (brute force, approximate)

Difficulty: Intermediate | Prerequisites: Basic RAG knowledge

Why it matters: Understanding vector DBs helps optimize RAG performance and cost

2. LLM Fine-Tuning

Connection to RAG: Complementary approaches often used together

What to learn:

  • Fine-tuning techniques (LoRA, QLoRA, full fine-tuning)
  • When to fine-tune vs use RAG
  • Dataset preparation for fine-tuning
  • Evaluation of fine-tuned models

Difficulty: Advanced | Prerequisites: ML fundamentals, PyTorch

Why it matters: Best systems combine RAG (facts) with fine-tuning (style/behavior)

3. Prompt Engineering

Connection to RAG: Critical for RAG prompt assembly

What to learn:

  • Prompt structures and patterns
  • Few-shot learning in prompts
  • Chain-of-thought prompting
  • Prompt optimization and testing

Difficulty: Beginner | Prerequisites: None

Why it matters: Better prompts = better RAG outputs, regardless of other components

4. Knowledge Graphs

Connection to RAG: Graph RAG combines both approaches

What to learn:

  • Graph databases (Neo4j, Amazon Neptune)
  • Knowledge graph construction
  • Graph traversal algorithms
  • Graph embeddings and node2vec

Difficulty: Advanced | Prerequisites: Graph theory concepts

Why it matters: Graph RAG handles complex relationships better than vector-only RAG

5. MLOps for LLMs

Connection to RAG: Production deployment and maintenance

What to learn:

  • LLMOps pipelines and infrastructure
  • Monitoring and observability
  • Continuous evaluation and deployment
  • Model versioning and A/B testing

Difficulty: Intermediate | Prerequisites: DevOps basics

Why it matters: RAG systems need production-grade MLOps for reliability

6. Information Retrieval

Connection to RAG: RAG is built on IR foundations

What to learn:

  • Classic IR algorithms (BM25, TF-IDF)
  • Evaluation metrics (precision, recall, MAP, NDCG)
  • Query understanding and expansion
  • Search relevance tuning

Difficulty: Intermediate | Prerequisites: None

Why it matters: RAG retrieval is just modern IR - foundations still apply

7. Data Engineering

Connection to RAG: Pipelines move data from source to vector DB

What to learn:

  • ETL/ELT pipelines
  • Stream processing (Kafka, Kinesis)
  • Data quality and validation
  • Orchestration (Airflow, Prefect)

Difficulty: Intermediate | Prerequisites: SQL, programming

Why it matters: RAG systems need robust data pipelines to stay current
